Wisp — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 June 2026 · Publisher: Lowtide

Wisp is a browser extension that suspends inactive tabs to free up memory. It is private by design.

The short version

Wisp does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal data. Everything Wisp does happens locally in your browser. There are no accounts, no logins, no analytics, no trackers, and the extension makes no network requests of its own.

What Wisp accesses — and why (all on your device)

  • Your open tabs (title, URL, audible state): to decide which inactive tabs to put to sleep and to never sleep a tab you are using. This never leaves your device.
  • Page content, via a content script on the sites you visit: only to detect whether a tab is playing media or has unsaved form input (so it is not slept), and to restore your scroll position and typed text when a slept tab wakes. Wisp does not read, store, or transmit the contents of your pages. Passwords are never captured.
  • Local storage (chrome.storage): to keep your settings and your never-sleep list on your device.

What Wisp does NOT do

  • No data collection, telemetry, or analytics.
  • No server or network communication initiated by the extension.
  • No accounts or sign-in.
  • No advertising, tracking, or fingerprinting.
  • No selling, sharing, or transferring of data (there is none to share).

Permissions

Wisp requests tabs, alarms, storage, contextMenus, and host access to web pages solely to provide its single function — sleeping inactive tabs locally. None of these are used to gather data about you.

Changes

If this policy changes, the “Last updated” date above will change. Material changes will be reflected in the extension's store listing.
